You can find public-domain, plain-text (or easily convertible) Strong’s Hebrew dictionary files,
often called "StrongsHebrew," from several open-source repositories and Bible study tools.
 
Here are the best sources:

* Open Scriptures (GitHub): The most reliable source for plain text or XML formats of Strong’s Hebrew lexicon.
  * Repository: openscriptures/strongs (contains dictionaries of Hebrew and Greek).
  * Lexicon Data: openscriptures/HebrewLexicon contains
    * Strong’s Hebrew dictionary (HebrewStrong.xml)
    * Brown-Driver-Briggs (BrownDriverBriggs.xml).
* SWORD Project (CrossWire Bible Society): Offers a downloadable, public-domain module named "StrongsHebrew".
* BibleSupport.com: Hosts a "Hebrew Lexicon 2.0" (Strong's + BDB) that is in the public domain
  and frequently used in software like e-Sword.
* Wikisource: The 1890 Strong's Exhaustive Concordance is in the public domain and available for digital text access.
* Scribd / Bibles Net: You can find PDF versions of the Strong's Hebrew dictionary which can be easily converted to plain text. 

License & Usage

* Public Domain: The original 1890 text of Strong's Hebrew dictionary is in the public domain.
